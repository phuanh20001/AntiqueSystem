Ming Dynasty Blue & White Porcelain Vase
✓ Verified Authentic
A magnificent example of Ming Dynasty blue-and-white porcelain, produced at the imperial kilns of Jingdezhen during the Xuande period. The vase features a classic meiping form with cobalt blue underglaze decoration depicting dragons chasing a flaming pearl among scrolling clouds. The base bears a six-character Xuande reign mark in underglaze blue.

Ming Dynasty Blue & White Imperial Kilns Meiping Form
Expert Assessment — Dr. A. Chen, PhD The item exhibits all hallmarks of authentic Xuande-period production: characteristic orange-peel texture of the glaze, heaped and piled effect in cobalt decoration, and precisely proportioned form consistent with imperial ware. Thermoluminescence dating confirms manufacture between 1400–1500 CE. No evidence of restoration or later reworking detected.
