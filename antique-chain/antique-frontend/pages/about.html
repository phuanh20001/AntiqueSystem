How the Blockchain Works

When a verifier approves an antique, our system takes a cryptographic snapshot of the entire verification record — the item details, the verifier's identity, the score, the timestamp, and the previous block's hash.

This snapshot is processed through the SHA-256 algorithm to produce a unique 64-character hash. That hash becomes the foundation of the next block — creating an unbreakable chain.

If even a single character of any past record were changed, the hash would change completely and the chain would be invalidated — making fraud instantly detectable.
